# Bill S-246: An Act respecting the recognition of wartime service

**Sponsor**: Senator YUSSUFF

## Summary

This enactment provides for the development of a national framework for the recognition of certain military service as wartime service and for the designation by the Governor in Council of military service as wartime service if it meets the objective criteria set out in the national framework.

Whereas Canadian Armed Forces members and veterans have served Canada in modern conflicts that involved significant risk, hardship and sacrifice, with resulting effects on them that have often endured long after their military service has ended;

Whereas recognition of wartime service should reflect the realities faced by those who served and should be grounded in fairness, consistency and respect for their military service;

Whereas Parliament has previously examined gaps and inconsistencies in recognition of modern military service, including through the work of the House of Commons Standing Committee on Veterans Affairs;

Whereas the recognition of wartime service has not always kept pace with the evolving nature of modern military operations;

Whereas many of Canadaâs closest allies have established clear and transparent ways of recognizing wartime service that are separate from benefits and other compensation;

And whereas it is appropriate for Parliament to put in place a clear and accountable process to ensure that wartime service is recognized fairly and consistently;

Now, therefore, His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:

## National Framework for the Recognition of Wartime Service

### Section 3

  **(1)** The Minister must develop a national framework.

  **(2)** The purpose of the national framework is to provide an accountable and publicly transparent process for the fair and meaningful symbolic and commemorative recognition of wartime service, based on the conditions of membersâ and veteransâ military service and on respect for their sacrifices.

  **(3)** The national framework must include

    objective criteria for designating military service as wartime service;

    fair and meaningful symbolic and commemorative measures to recognize wartime service; and

    measures to recognize nonâCanadian Armed Forces personnel who have served alongside or in support of a Canadian Armed Forces operation.

  **(4)** The objective criteria developed under paragraph (3)(a) must consider the conditions in which military service was performed, including

    the level of risk that members and veterans faced during their service, including exposure to hostile or life-threatening conditions;

    the nature, rhythm, duration, scale and intensity of the operations members and veterans carried out during their service;

    the exposure of members and veterans to conditions capable of causing physical or psychological injury during their service; and

    the existence of an international or non-international armed conflict in the geographic location where the member or veteran served, regardless of a declared state of war.

  **(5)** The objective criteria developed under paragraph (3)(a) may not consider the formal label or description applied to military service at the time it was performed.

  **(6)** To develop the national framework, the Minister must consult with

    the Minister of Veterans Affairs;

    veterans and representatives of groups that advocate on behalf of veterans;

    nonâCanadian Armed Forces personnel or former personnel who have served alongside or in support of a Canadian Armed Forces operation, or representatives of groups that advocate on their behalf;

    persons with recognized expertise in military history or other relevant expertise; and

    any other person or entity that the Minister considers appropriate.


## Reports to Parliament

### Section 4

  **(1)** Within one year after the day on which this Act comes into force, the Minister must prepare a report setting out the national framework and cause it to be tabled in each House of Parliament on any of the first 15 days on which that House is sitting after the report is completed.

  **(2)** The report must include a summary of the views received during the consultations referred to in subsection 3(6) and explain how those views were considered in the development of the national framework.

  **(3)** The Minister must publish the report on the Department of National Defence website within 10 days after the day on which the report is tabled in Parliament.

### Section 5

  **(1)** Within 5 years after the day on which the national framework is tabled in Parliament, and every 10 years after that, the Minister must prepare a report setting out

    the measures from the national framework that have been implemented and their effectiveness in providing fair and meaningful symbolic and commemorative recognition of wartime service;

    with respect to any measure included in the national framework that has not been implemented, the reason it has not been implemented and the timeline for implementing the measure;

    any new measures taken by the Minister that are consistent with the goals of the national framework; and

    a list of the objective criteria for designating military service as wartime service.

  **(2)** In the report prepared under subsection (1), the Minister may add or remove objective criteria for designating military service as wartime service under the national framework after consulting with the persons and entities referred to in subsection 3(6).

  **(3)** The Minister must cause the report to be tabled in each House of Parliament on any of the first 15 days on which that House is sitting after the report is completed.

  **(4)** The Minister must publish the report on the Department of National Defence website within 10 days after the day on which the report is tabled in Parliament.
