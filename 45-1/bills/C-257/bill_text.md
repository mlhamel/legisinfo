# Bill C-257: An Act to amend the Criminal Code (promotion of terrorist activity or group)

## Summary

This enactment amends the `Criminal Code` to create an offence of wilfully promoting a terrorist activity or a terrorist group, or any activity of a terrorist group.

His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:

### Section 2

The heading before section 83.18 of the Act is replaced by the following:


## **Promoting**, Participating, Facilitating, Instructing and Harbouring

### Section 83.171

  **(1)** Every person who, by communicating statements, wilfully promotes any terrorist activity or terrorist group, or any activity of a terrorist group, is guilty of an indictable offence and liable to imprisonment for a term of not more than five years.

  **(2)** An offence may be committed under subsection (1) whether or not the statements communicated by the accused

    specifically or generally relate to a terrorist activity or terrorist group, or to an activity of a terrorist group; or

    actually encouraged or instigated any person to participate in a terrorist activity or in an activity of a terrorist group.

  **(3)** No person shall be convicted of an offence under subsection (1) if

    they establish that the statements communicated were true;

    in good faith, they expressed or attempted to establish by an argument an opinion on a religious subject or an opinion based on a belief in a religious text;

    the statements were relevant to any subject of public interest, the discussion of which was for the public benefit, and they believe on reasonable grounds that the statements are true; or

    in good faith, they intended to point out, for the purpose of removal, matters promoting or tending to promote a terrorist activity or terrorist group, or an activity of a terrorist group.

  **(4)** In this section, **communicating** and **statements** have the same meaning as in subsection 319(7).
