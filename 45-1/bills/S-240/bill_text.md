# Bill S-240: An Act to amend the Criminal Code (declaration of exception pursuant to subsection 33(1) of the Charter for mandatory minimum sentences for child sexual abuse and exploitation material offences)

**Sponsor**: Senator HOUSAKOS

## Summary

This enactment amends section 163.1 of the `Criminal Code` to declare that paragraphs 163.1(4)(a) and 163.1(4.1)(a) â which include mandatory minimum sentences for the offences of possession of child sexual abuse and exploitation material and accessing child sexual abuse and exploitation material, respectively â operate notwithstanding section 12 of the `Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms`.

His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:


## `Criminal Code`

### Section 1

Section 163.1 of the `Criminal Code` is amended by adding the following after subsection (4.1):

**(4.11)** Pursuant to subsection 33(1) of the `Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms`, paragraphs 163.1(4)(a) and 163.1(4.1)(a) of this Act are declared to operate notwithstanding section 12 of the `Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms`.
