# Bill C-260: An Act to amend the Criminal Code (medical assistance in dying â protection against coercion)

## Summary

This enactment amends the `Criminal Code` to make it an offence for certain persons in certain circumstances to initiate a discussion with a person about the availability to them of medical assistance in dying, knowing that they have not specifically requested to have such a discussion.

His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:


## Short Title

### Section 1

This Act may be cited as the `Preventing Coercion of Persons Not Seeking Medical Assistance in Dying Act`.


## Criminal Code

### Section 2

The `Criminal Code` is amended by adding the following after section 241.3:

### Section 241.301

Every person who, being an officer or employee of the Government of Canada or of a province, other than a medical practitioner or a registered nurse, including a nurse practitioner, initiates a discussion with a person about the availability to that person of medical assistance in dying is guilty of an offence punishable on summary conviction if they

by virtue of their profession, are in a position of trust or authority towards that person; and

know that the person has not specifically requested to have such a discussion with them.
