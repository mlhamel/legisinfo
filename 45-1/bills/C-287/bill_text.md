# Bill C-287: An Act to amend the National Research Council Act

## Summary

This enactment amends the `National Research Council Act` to provide that, in its role supporting the national model code development system, the National Research Council must prepare, maintain and publish the following:

standardized housing cost and impact summaries for material housing-related model code proposals to be used in the public review of those proposals;

governance transparency information respecting such proposals, including relevant committee memberships, roles and institutional or organizational affiliations; and

an accessible public registry of such proposals and associated materials that includes edition-level and annual summaries of code development activities and impacts.

His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, enacts as follows:


## Short Title

### Section 1

This Act may be cited as the `Housing Cost Transparency Act`.

### Section 4

The Act is amended by adding the following after section 5.1:

### Section 5.2

  **(1)** The Council must prepare, maintain and publish on its website, in both official languages and in an accessible format, a housing cost and impact summary for each material housing-related model code proposal.

  **(2)** The housing cost and impact summary, along with any relevant materials, must be published when the proposal is first made available for public review.

  **(3)** The housing cost and impact summary must include the following information about the proposal:

    the nature and intent of the proposal and the problem it is intended to address;

    a plain-language summary of the proposal, including what is its practical effect, its estimated costs for a typical house or other representative dwelling unit, such as individual single-family homes and multiple unit residential complexes, and its main expected benefits and drawbacks;

    any expected implications of the proposal for residential construction costs, timing and complexity and for the administrative burden on residential construction;

    a detailed quantitative estimate of the incremental initial costs, material operating costs and savings and other quantifiable benefits of the proposal, which uses ranges and clearly stated assumptions where quantification is possible, including the detailed costs for a typical house or other representative dwelling unit, such as individual single-family homes and multiple unit residential complexes;

    the party who is expected to bear the costs referred to in paragraph (d);

    the main uncertainties, data limitations and underlying assumptions that affect the estimate referred to in paragraph (d); and

    the name of every individual recorded by the Council as having initiated, requested, sponsored, submitted or formally advanced the proposal on their own behalf or on behalf of a body, committee, organization, association, government department or other entity, together with each individual芒聙聶s relevant committee membership position, role and institutional or organizational affiliation at the time of that action.

  **(4)** If it is not possible for the Council to provide a detailed quantitative estimate of the costs of the material housing-related model code proposal under paragraph (3)(d), the Council must provide the best available qualitative description of the expected costs, together with an explanation as to why a quantitative estimate is not practicable in the circumstances.

### Section 5.3

For each material housing-related model code proposal, the Council must prepare, maintain and publish on its website, in both official languages and in an accessible format

the list of relevant governance bodies and committees involved in the proposal, including their members芒聙聶 name, position, role and, where appropriate, institutional or organizational affiliation; and

the point of contact for technical and process-related questions during the public review of the proposal.

### Section 5.4

The Council must prepare, maintain and publish on its website, in both official languages and in an accessible format, a public registry of material housing-related model code proposals that contains

the housing cost and impact summary 芒聙聰 including key process milestones achieved during the public review of the proposal 芒聙聰 list of relevant governance bodies and committees 芒聙聰 including the governance body or committee responsible for each proposal 芒聙聰 publication date, version date and up-to-date status information for each material housing-related model code proposal;

for each new national model code edition, an estimate of the combined net impact of the material housing-related model code proposals incorporated into that edition for a typical house or other representative dwelling unit, such as individual single-family homes and multiple unit residential complexes; and

an annual summary describing the national model codes developed or updated, the number and types of proposals received and any recurring issues encountered in estimating costs and relating to process transparency.

### Section 5.5

A failure by the Council to publish, maintain or update the housing cost and impact summaries under section 5.2, the list of relevant governance bodies and committees under section 5.3 or the public registry, including annual summaries, under section 5.4 does not invalidate any national model code or any action or legislative measure taken by provinces, territories or municipal or local authorities in relation to a national model code.
